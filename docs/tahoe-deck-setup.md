# Tahoe Deck — Getting Started (≈15 minutes)

The app is at **https://contractor-north.vercel.app** — works in any browser; on a phone, "Add to Home Screen" to install it like an app.

Your company's data is completely separate from any other company on the system — walled off at the database level.

---

## 1. Create your company (Chris — 5 min)
1. Open the app → **Sign up** with your email + a password.
2. It'll ask you to create a company. Enter:
   - **Company name:** `Tahoe Deck`
   - **Your trade:** choose **Deck / carpentry** ← this gives you deck job codes (Demo, Footings, Framing, Decking, Railing, Stairs, Finish/Seal, Repair, etc.) instead of generic ones.
3. **Settings → Company:** add your **General‑B license #**, address, phone, and logo.

## 2. Add your crew (Chris — 5 min)
**Settings → Team → Add employee** for each of the ~10 guys:
- Enter their **name + email** and choose role **Tech** (field employee).
- The app shows a **password once** — text it to that employee.
- They open the app on their phone, log in, "Add to Home Screen," and they're ready.

> Office staff (you + your secretary) should be role **Owner** or **Office** — they can edit timecards. Techs cannot.

## 3. Bring in your customers (5 min)
Export your existing clients to a **CSV** (name, address, phone, email). In the app: **Customers → Import CSV** and map the columns.
*(Send Erik the file if you'd rather we run it for you.)*

## How the timeclock works for your guys (the part that bit you before)
Employees (techs) are **locked down** so wrong hours can't reach the wrong jobs:
- ⏱️ **Start time** — they can only round the live clock‑in **back to the nearest half hour**. No arbitrary times, no backdating.
- 🚫 **No padding** — they **cannot** edit a past entry or add manual hours. You or your secretary make any corrections.
- 📍 **GPS** is stamped on every clock‑in and clock‑out, visible to the office.
- 🧰 The **job** they clock into is limited to their assigned jobs.

You, your secretary, and Erik keep full editing on every timecard.
